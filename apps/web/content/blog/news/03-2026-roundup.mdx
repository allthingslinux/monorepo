# March 2026 Roundup

March focused on practical tooling, open-source ecosystem updates, and community security awareness. We shipped several new utilities on `atl.tools` and continued sharing high-signal policy and security notices with members.

## atl.tools Platform Updates

We rolled out a refreshed landing page and open-sourced the project:

- `atl.tools` directory/landing updates
- Repository published at `github.com/allthingslinux/atl.tools`

New tools released this month:

- **hn.atl.tools** - cleaner Hacker News reading experience
- **json.atl.tools** - visualize and work with JSON/YAML/XML/CSV
- **pdf.atl.tools** - self-hosted PDF utility toolkit

## Tux Improvements

Tux received additional command updates in March, continuing iterative quality-of-life improvements following the v0.1 rollout period.

## Privacy and Policy Awareness

We continued sharing updates on major privacy policy developments and age-verification legislation affecting online platforms.

This included:

- EU Chat Control voting awareness updates
- Clarification posts to reduce misinformation around age-verification changes and related FOSS concerns

## Security Signals Shared

ATL continued forwarding relevant security advisories and incident signals for member awareness, including distro/platform-linked notices when applicable.

## Looking Ahead

March was a steady execution month: more useful public tools, continued transparency in platform direction, and ongoing security/privacy communication to help our community stay informed and resilient.
