# February 2026 Roundup

February was one of ATL's biggest milestone months to date. We crossed 20,000 members, expanded transparency efforts, and launched a public mirror network to better support Linux and FOSS distribution access.

## 20,000 Members

In just nine months after reaching 10K, ATL crossed **20,000 members**.

Thank you to everyone who contributes through support, moderation, wiki editing, development, operations, and day-to-day community participation. Every contribution matters.

## Radical Transparency: `/open` and 990-N

This month we reinforced our transparency model with major public reporting improvements:

- Filed our first **990-N** after completing our first financial year as a 501(c)(3)
- Launched **`/open`** with a public financial ledger at `allthingslinux.org/open`
- Expanded visibility into donations and expenses as part of our transparency commitment
- Promoted contribution pathways via `allthingslinux.org/contribute`

## ATL Mirror Network Launch

We officially launched ATL mirrors for distributions, repositories, and projects.

Current rollout details:

- Public index: `mirror.allthingslinux.org`
- Stats page: `mirror.allthingslinux.org/stats`
- Initial deployment in Quebec with approximately **8TB** capacity and **1Gbps** uplink
- Expansion planning already in progress for additional locations

## Service Operations

We performed several routine config and reliability updates across services during the month and communicated expected intermittent downtime windows as needed.

We also published a community PSA regarding Discord's upcoming age-verification
changes for NSFW contexts, including scam-awareness guidance and reminders to
only trust official verification channels.

## Looking Ahead

With 20K members reached, transparency systems shipped, and mirrors now live, ATL enters the next phase focused on scaling infrastructure, improving service resilience, and broadening public resources for the Linux/FOSS ecosystem.
