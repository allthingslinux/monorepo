## Key Patterns Across Tildes

### Governance
- **Anarcho-monarchist**: tilde.town — most scalable for general communities
- **Single admin**: tilde.team — works, but document everything
- **Non-profit**: sdf.org, arbornet.org — adds legitimacy + tax benefits
- **Succession**: thunix.net (3 transitions) — plan before you need it
- **Graceful closure**: grex.org — archive content, announce early, set end date

### Infrastructure Choices
- **Ansible everywhere**: dimension.sh, Project Segfault — IaC is non-negotiable for longevity
- **Open source infra**: vern.cc (all scripts public) — builds trust, enables contributions
- **Git-based deploy**: tilde.cafe — no direct edits, everything through git
- **Centralized identity**: tilde.green (LLDAP + Keycloak) — for multi-service SSO
- **Subdomain-per-user**: envs.net, remotes.club — professional URLs, worth setup cost

### Defunct Tildes (What Didn't Survive the 2014 Wave)

The 2014 tilde wave spawned dozens of servers. Most are gone. Notable examples:

- **nand.club** — retrocomputing focus (defunct)
- **RIOTGIRL.CLUB** — feminist safe space (defunct)
- **club6.nl** — first IPv6-only pubnix, Amsterdam; also ran IRC/NNTP (defunct)
- **oldbsd.club** — retro BSDs and historic Unices (defunct)
- **tilde.farm** — Gentoo Linux + Puppet, mission around "unstable environments" (defunct)
- **squiggle.city** — organized around a specific book ("userland") (defunct)

**The pattern**: Creative identity alone doesn't sustain a tilde. Operational sustainability — regular admin attention, community maintenance, clear funding — matters more than a unique concept. The graveyard of defunct tildes is full of interesting ideas run by one person who burned out.

**Use pfhawkins' lists**: tilde.club/~pfhawkins/othertildes.html (active, JSON API available) and tilde.club/~pfhawkins/defuncttildes.html to study what survived vs. what didn't.

### Unique Differentiators That Worked
1. **IBM i/AS400** (pub400.com) — 70,000 users through pure platform uniqueness
2. **Gemini birthplace** (zaibatsu) — protocol origin story becomes identity
3. **SSH registration** (tilde.fun) — automated, filters for SSH-capable users
4. **Anarcho-monarchist governance** (tilde.town) — named model attracts aligned users
5. **Sci-fi collaborative universe** (cosmic.voyage) — thematic creates tight community
6. **Transparent donations** (crime.team) — builds trust through radical openness
7. **Hyper-specific theme** (breadpunk.club) — even baking can anchor a tilde community
8. **Nostalgia rebuild** (dimension.sh) — nikdoof recreated a defunct 1997 college pubnix (dimension.spodnet.uk.com) to reconnect the old community. Building around an existing social history is a valid and underused founding approach.
